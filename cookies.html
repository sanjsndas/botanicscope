1. What Are Cookies?

Cookies are small data files sent to your browser from a website and stored on your device. They collect information about your browsing behavior and preferences.

They allow websites to recognize you, store preferences, and tailor content to enhance your experience.

Cookies can be first-party or third-party, depending on who sets them.

They do not contain personally identifiable information but store unique identifiers to link browser sessions.

Cookies can have different lifespans, such as session cookies (temporary) or persistent cookies (longer-term).

Many websites rely on cookies to provide rich, interactive functionality.

Cookies are safe and widely adopted technology.

Disabling cookies might hinder full website functionality or login persistence.

Learn how cookies work helps you make informed decisions about your data online.

BotaniScope uses cookies responsibly to balance experience with privacy needs.
